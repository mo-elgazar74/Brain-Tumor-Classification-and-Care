about us

The brain tumor project entailed a comprehensive approach, involving the utilization of Kaggle, GitHub, and extensive research from various sources, including online searches. The primary objective was to develop an artificial intelligence-based solution for the swift and accurate diagnosis of brain tumors. Extensive model exploration and dataset analysis were conducted to ensure comprehensive coverage and high accuracy.

Going beyond diagnosis, the project aimed to improve outcomes in the fields of neurology and oncology. By leveraging machine learning algorithms trained on diverse medical imaging data, the project sought to enhance diagnostic precision, enabling early detection of brain tumors. The ultimate goal was to make a positive impact on patient care and support their after-care programs. In addition to accurate diagnosis, the project explored the development of an AI-generated Chatbot named llama3. This Chatbot aimed to provide personalized support to patients, assisting them in their after-care journey. By combining the power of artificial intelligence with comprehensive datasets and model exploration, the project aimed to revolutionize the field of brain tumor diagnosis and improve patient care.

stories

2018 - 2020

Ben Roche: The Angry Gamer's Triumph Over Brain Cancer.

Ben Roche, known as "The Angry Gamer," was a popular YouTuber who survived a glioblastoma multiforme brain tumor diagnosed in 2018. Despite the aggressive treatment, he continued creating content, inspiring and educating millions about brain cancer. By 2020, Ben celebrated five years tumor-free, using his platform to support others facing similar challenges.

2015 - 2018

Josh's Journey: From Brain Tumor Diagnosis to Advocacy

Diagnosed with an anaplastic oligodendroglioma at 24, Josh documented his treatment journey in a book to raise awareness about brain cancer. He founded the non-profit "Brain Tumor Awareness" in 2015, becoming a key advocate despite a tumor recurrence in 2018. Josh's resilience and advocacy have inspired many and bolstered support for brain cancer research.

1999 - Present

AnnaBelle's Resilience: Living with Glioblastoma

Diagnosed with glioblastoma multiforme in 1999, AnnaBelle Giles shares her two-decade journey through a popular blog and book, inspiring others with her resilience and advocacy for brain cancer awareness. Despite surgeries, treatments, and complications, she continues to advocate, motivating countless others to embrace life fully.
